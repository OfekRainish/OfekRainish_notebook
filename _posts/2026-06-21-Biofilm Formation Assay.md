# Biofilm Formation Assay

## Overview

Biofilm formation was quantified using a crystal violet staining assay in 96-well microtiter plates.

---

## Experimental Setup

Each plate contained the following conditions:

* **Replicates** of *Paenibacillus dendritiformis* grown in **LB medium + 6.25 µM surfactin**
* **Replicates** of *Paenibacillus dendritiformis* grown in **LB medium without surfactin**
* **Replicates** of *Bacillus subtilis* as a **positive control**
* **Same amount of wells** containing **sterile LB medium** as a **negative control**

**Final volume per well:** `200 µL`

    *All wells were treated either with surfactin or with methanol and UPW as control. These volumes were added to the wells before the bacteria cultures and allowed to evaporate. For surfacin wells: 32.5 µL of 0.1 surfactin in methanol solution. For control: 32.5 µL of a solution containing 90% metanol and 10% UPW. 
---

## Procedure

### 1. Plate inoculation

Prepare a 96-well microtiter plate with the conditions listed above, ensuring a final volume of **200 µL** in each well.

### 2. Initial incubation

Incubate the plate **overnight at 30°C with shaking at 200 rpm**.

> To minimize evaporation, place the plate in a **closed container containing wet paper** during incubation.

### 3. Static incubation for biofilm formation

After the overnight incubation:

* **stop shaking**
* continue incubating the plate **statically at 30°C**
* allow biofilm formation for the wanted period of time.

### 4. Removal of planktonic cells

Following incubation, carefully remove the planktonic culture from each well.

### 5. Washing

Gently wash the wells with **distilled water** to remove remaining non-adherent cells.

### 6. Crystal violet staining

Add **100 µL of 0.4% (w/v) crystal violet solution** to each well *.

* Incubate for **1 min**

    *C.V powder diluted in sterile water (post autoclave).
### 7. Remove excess stain

Remove the crystal violet solution and gently wash the wells with **distilled water** to eliminate excess stain.

### 8. Solubilization of bound crystal violet

Add **100 µL of 100% ethanol** to each well to solubilize the bound crystal violet.

### 9. Quantification

Measure absorbance at **595 nm** using a **microplate reader**.
