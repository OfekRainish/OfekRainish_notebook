## LC–MS Sample Preparation from *P. dendritiformis* Biomass

After growing *P. dendritiformis* with and without surfactin, samples were separated into **culture medium** and **biomass** fractions for independent LC–MS analysis.  
The separation was performed **immediately at the desired sampling time point (2, 12, or 20 hours)**, directly after growth and **before storage at 4°C or -80°C**.

### Biomass separation
1. Vortex the breathing tube thoroughly.
2. Transfer the culture to an Eppendorf tube.
3. Centrifuge at **0°C, 20,000 × g for 20 min**.
4. Carefully separate the **supernatant (medium fraction)** from the **pellet (biomass fraction)**.

---

## Biomass extraction for LC–MS
1. Add **100 µL cold methanol** to the biomass pellet.
2. Vortex for **1 min**.
3. Spin down briefly for **1 min**.
4. Sonicate the sample **on ice for 20 min at 40 kHz**.
5. Dry the tube and place the sample **overnight at -80°C**.
6. add water to get to a **50:50 methanol:water solution** :
   -  we already have added **100 µL methanol**
   - so add **100 µL LC–MS-grade water**
7. Vortex for **1 min**.
8. Centrifuge at **0°C, 20,000 × g for 20 min**.
9. Transfer **150 µL of the supernatant** to a clean Eppendorf tube.
10. Centrifuge again at **0°C, 20,000 × g for 20 min**.
11. Transfer **100 µL of the supernatant** to a **glass LC–MS vial with an insert**.
12. If needed, gently tap the vial on the bench to remove air bubbles before analysis.
